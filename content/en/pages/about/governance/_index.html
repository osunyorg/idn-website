The IDN has adopted sociocracy as its overall governing structure based on the following principles:

header_text: >- header_cta: display: false label: >- target: "" external: false contents_reading_time: seconds: 88 text: >- 1 minute contents: - kind: block template: title title: >- 1. The Principle of Circles slug: >- 1-the-principle-of-circles ranks: base: 2 self: 2 top: active: true title: value: >- 1. The Principle of Circles heading: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: base: 3 top: active: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

The governance structure of the IDN shall consist of numerous working groups or organisations called Circles. A Circle is defined as a group of people fulfilling specific roles and cooperating for a common purpose. All Circles shall have a clearly defined purpose, responsibilities, and domain and should be double-linked to at least one other Circle.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- 2. The Principle of Consent slug: >- 2-the-principle-of-consent ranks: base: 2 self: 2 top: active: true title: value: >- 2. The Principle of Consent heading: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: base: 3 top: active: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

All policy decisions, including the selection of people for Circle roles, shall be made through a process of consent that involves those directly affected. Achieving consent shall be conducted in a way to ensure that all members’ input is heard (e.g. via rounds). Members consent to a decision when there are no reasoned objections to a proposal.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- 3. The Principle of Equivalence slug: >- 3-the-principle-of-equivalence ranks: base: 2 self: 2 top: active: true title: value: >- 3. The Principle of Equivalence heading: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: base: 3 top: active: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

Making and reviewing decisions shall involve people who are affected by them. All Circles, and their members, shall have an equal say, and any member of a Circle may make a proposal.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- 4. The Principle of Continuous Evolution slug: >- 4-the-principle-of-continuous-evolution ranks: base: 2 self: 2 top: active: true title: value: >- 4. The Principle of Continuous Evolution heading: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: base: 3 top: active: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

The IDN as a whole, and each Circle in particular, shall be committed to ongoing feedback and continual learning about governance, communication, and the content of the Circles’ work. All policy decisions, including selections of people for roles, shall be scheduled for feedback and review after a deﬁned period of time (its term) and may be re-evaluated at any time. Circles and members are also encouraged to test new proposals through experimentation. 

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- 5. The Principle of Transparency slug: >- 5-the-principle-of-transparency ranks: base: 2 self: 2 top: active: true title: value: >- 5. The Principle of Transparency heading: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: base: 3 top: active: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

Circles shall record all information that is valuable for the IDN and make it accessible to all IDN members unless the Circle determines that there is a good reason for confidentiality.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- 6. The Principle of Effectiveness slug: >- 6-the-principle-of-effectiveness ranks: base: 2 self: 2 top: active: true title: value: >- 6. The Principle of Effectiveness heading: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: base: 3 top: active: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

Circles should devote time only to what brings the Circle and its members closer to achieving the purpose of the Circle.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- 7. The Principle of Accountability slug: >- 7-the-principle-of-accountability ranks: base: 2 self: 2 top: active: true title: value: >- 7. The Principle of Accountability heading: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: base: 3 top: active: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

Circles and their members should consider their capacities before taking on a new responsibility, so they are able to fulfil tasks they committed to. They should strive to improve the IDN and take responsibility for their work and the IDN as a whole.
